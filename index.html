Mingyu Yang

PhD Candidate
Harvard-MIT Health Sciences & Technology

I’m Ming, a fourth-year PhD Candidate at Harvard-MIT Health Sciences & Technology. I work in the Van Vliet Group for Material Chemomechanics, where I use 3D-printing technologies to engineer in vitro models of myelination. I also collaborate with the Molecular Biology Education Research Group at Rochester Institute of Technology to study how biology students learn about chemistry concepts.

Outside of the lab, I develop massive open online courses (MOOCs) about biology and enginering, and I co-chair the Professional Development committee of DBER-SiT, an organization that supports trainees in discipline-based education research (DBER). I care deeply about teaching, mentorship, and democratizing STEM education, and I hope to eventually pursue a teaching-focused faculty position.
